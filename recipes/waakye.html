Auntie Mamuna's Waakye Recipe

Description

Waakye is a popular Ghanaian dish made of rice and beans cooked with millet leaves (waakye leaves) for added color and flavor

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Black-eyed peas or Red Cowpeas
  • Waakye Leaves
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Bicarbonate of soda (baking soda)

Steps

  1. Rinse the beans thoroughly.
  2. In a large pot, add the beans, waakye leaves, and enough water to cover the beans by about an inch.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the beans are tender but not mushy.
  4. If you're using baking soda instead of waakye leaves, add it at this point to enhance the color.
  5. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear, then add it to the pot with the beans.
  6. Add salt to taste and mix gently.
  7. Add more water if necessary, so the rice and beans are covered by about an inch of water.
  8. Cover the pot and let it cook on medium heat for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the water. Stir occasionally to prevent burning, but avoid over-stirring as it can make the rice mushy.
  9. Once the rice is tender and water is absorbed, remove from heat and let it sit covered for 5-10 minutes.
  10. Remove the waakye leaves before serving.
  11. Serve waakye hot with your favorite sides, such as fried plantains, boiled eggs, spaghetti, fish, or a Ghanaian tomato stew.
