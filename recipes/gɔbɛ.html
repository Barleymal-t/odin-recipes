Hajia Salia's Gɔbɛ

Description

Gɔbɛ, also known as Gari and Beans, a beloved Ghanaian street food dish made with beans, palm oil, and gari (cassava flour). It's typically served with fried ripe plantains, avocado, and sometimes boiled eggs.

Ingredients

  • Black-eyed peas or Red Cowpeas
  • Palm oil
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Pepper (scotch bonnet or chili)
  • Salt
  • Gari
  • Ripe plantains
  • Avocado
  • Boiled eggs

Steps

  1. Rinse the beans thoroughly.
  2. In a large pot, add the beans and enough water to cover them by about an inch.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the beans are tender. Add salt to taste in the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  4. Drain the water and set the beans aside.
  5. In a saucepan, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Be careful not to overheat as it can burn quickly.
  6. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become soft and translucent.
  7. Add the chopped tomatoes and pepper, and cook until the tomatoes break down, forming a thick sauce.
  8. Add salt to taste.
  9. Add the cooked beans to the palm oil sauce and stir to combine, allowing the beans to absorb the flavors of the sauce. Cook for a few more minutes, then remove from heat.
  10. Place the bean mixture on a plate and sprinkle a generous amount of gari over the beans.
  11. Serve with fried ripe plantains, sliced avocado, and boiled eggs if desired.
